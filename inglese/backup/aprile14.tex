\s[Oscar Wilde]
\ss[Life]
He was an Irish author, but spent a lot of time in England \t until he was 20 he lived in Dublin, where he was born in 1884 from a middleclass family \t father doctor, mother intellectual \\
Both his parents were Irish independentists \t they believed in Ireland independence (was a colony, and only in 1921 Ireland achieved independence) \\
His parents were fervant nationalists, however he had not that political commitment \\
He attended Ireland's most prestigious university (Trinity college) \t once he had graduated, he moved to England because of studying reasons \t he attended Oxford university \\
Here he studied greek, latin and foreign languages \t after graduating from Oxford, he remained and moved in London, where he became a celebrety because of:
\bi
  \item his literary works
  \item for his lifestyle
\ei
He had an eccentric lifestyle \t he was a dandy: he wore eccentric and colorful clothes, and was a sophisticated speaker \t he used wit exstensevly \t he was also very sensitive to beauty and art \\
He became so famous that he was invited to the USA (like Dickens) to hold conferences there

\v

He got married and had two children \t but he started a homosexual relationship with a young man, who belonged to one of the most powerful families in England \t Lord Alfred Douglas, son of Marquis of Queensberry \\
When Marquis of Queensberry knew this, publicly accused Oscar Wilde of homosexuality (which was prohibited) \\
Wilde brought him to court and a trial for defamation started \t but the marquis showed evidences and brough witnesses \t so the marquis was declared not guilty, and two other trials against Wilde started \t he was accused of obscene acts \\
In this moment, Wilde was actually at the peak of his popularity \t so the trials were exstensevly covered by the english press \\
He was mainly a playwright \\
In the end he was declared guilty \t this was a blow to his reputation and career (all his plays got canceled) \t he was sentenced to hard labor \\
As he was released, he moved to Paris where he lived in poverty, and died in the year 1900 \\
Additionally, his wife and children did not want to have anything to do with him \t the two children declared that they would have joined the war to was away the shame that Oscar Wilde had brought to their family
